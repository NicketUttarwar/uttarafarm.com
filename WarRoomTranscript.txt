An Interview with Vijay Uttarwar
Nicket Uttarwar: Welcome, Dad, it's great to have you here. I'm excited to hear about your entrepreneurial journey with Naturell.
Vijay Uttarwar: Thanks, Nicket. It's a pleasure to be here and share my story.
Nicket: Let's start from the beginning. What inspired you to transition from a successful career in software to the consumer products industry, specifically with Stevia?
Vijay: It was a combination of factors. I always felt a pull towards contributing to the farming community, and Stevia seemed like a perfect opportunity. It promised higher incomes for farmers compared to traditional crops, and being a zero-calorie natural sweetener, it ticked the boxes for a healthy alternative. It felt like a good business proposition with a social cause - a way to improve the lives of farmers. Our initial tagline was “Wealthy Urban, Healty Rural”.
Nicket: You mentioned Stevia faced some regulatory hurdles early on. Could you elaborate on that?
Vijay: Yes, initially we outsourced manufacturing and secured the necessary licenses. However, a year in, the central government declared Stevia wasn’t on the approved list of sweeteners. It was a major setback. We had already built a team and invested resources. It would've been easy to give up, but my entrepreneurial spirit wouldn't let me. We had to pivot.
Nicket: That's when you decided to enter the nutrition bar market, right? What were the initial challenges?
Vijay: Absolutely. We launched Rite Bite bars priced at ₹15, targeting them as healthy chocolate for children. However, we quickly realized that ₹15 was a barrier for many consumers, and the positioning as "healthy chocolate" wasn't resonating. We learned that extensive consumer research was crucial, something we lacked initially. We reduced the price to ₹5, but faced another challenge - our premium product was placed in smaller stores where customers didn't understand the concept of a nutrition bar.
Nicket: It sounds like a constant learning process. How did you overcome these challenges and what led to the creation of Max Protein?
Vijay: We learned that clear communication was key. Since we didn't have the resources for large-scale consumer awareness campaigns, we decided to highlight the functional benefits directly on the packaging. We identified three target segments - gym-goers, calorie-conscious consumers, and women - and tailored our products accordingly. The "workout" branding for our protein bars clicked with gym-goers, especially when they realized they could get 20 grams of protein for a much lower price than imported brands. To cater to their demand for higher protein content, we launched Max Protein.
Nicket: It's impressive how you identified a niche and grew within that segment. What were the key factors that drove Naturell’s growth to a ₹170 crore business?
Vijay: Focusing on protein and fiber as primary benefits was crucial. We expanded Max Protein beyond bars, creating a platform for healthy snacks including protein chips, cookies, granola, peanut butter, and plant protein options. We constantly innovated, introducing new concepts like millet-based protein chips, a first in India and possibly globally.
Nicket: You mentioned aiming for a ₹1000 crore brand. What prompted the decision to partner with Zydus Wellness to achieve that goal?
Vijay: Building a brand from scratch to ₹25 crore is a different ball game than scaling it to ₹1000 crore. It requires more resources, expertise, and a shared passion. Zydus Wellness impressed me with their understanding of building and nurturing industries. They had created the sugar-free sweetener and face mask markets in India. Their existing health brand portfolio and resources combined with Naturell’s agility made for a compelling partnership.
Nicket: What were the key factors you considered when making this decision? Was it a difficult decision?
Vijay: It was a significant decision, but I focused on four key stakeholders: the brand, employees, shareholders, and partners. I wanted to ensure the brand’s continued growth and maintain the startup culture. For employees, job security and career growth opportunities were paramount. I also wanted all shareholders, including employees through ESOPs, to benefit financially. And lastly, I wanted to ensure our supply chain and distribution partners continued to thrive.
Nicket: That's commendable. You've always prioritized your employees. How did you ensure their well-being during the acquisition?
Vijay: I negotiated with Zydus Wellness to run Naturell as a separate independent subsidiary, guaranteeing no job losses. Additionally, employees received a higher share price than investors during the transaction. 50% of the payout was immediate, and the remaining 50% will be disbursed after a year with a 25% increase, ensuring their financial security. We even extended payouts to individuals not on our payroll, like promoters and support staff. Everyone who contributed to Naturell's success deserved to share in the rewards.
Nicket: It's inspiring to see your commitment to your team. What's next for you?
Vijay: I'll continue to be associated with Rite Bite and Max Protein in an advisory role. The brands will always be close to my heart. I'm also excited to explore new avenues. After 20 years in education, 20 years in software, and 20 years in the consumer business, I'm looking forward to the next 20 years, focusing on social causes and personal interests.
Nicket: Thank you for sharing your incredible journey, Dad. I'm proud of everything you've accomplished and excited to see what you do next.
Vijay: Thanks, Nicket. It's been a fulfilling journey, and I'm grateful for the opportunity to have made a difference.
